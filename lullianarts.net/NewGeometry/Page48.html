2.2.6 Center
Principles of the Center
   1. The center is the point in which lines converge the most closely.
   2. The center is the place where lines reach their greatest perfection.
   3. Nowhere is the point as perfect as in the center.
   4. A figure cannot be without a center.
   5. Not all centers are located here below.
   6. Gravity seeks one center and levity seeks another.
   7. There is no locus as naturally desirable as the center.
   8. Every center is common.
   9. One who destroys the center also destroys motion.
  10. All the secondary acts of powers are likenesses of the center.

Corollaries to the Principles of the Center
   1. It was said that the center is the point in which lines converge the most closely. Hence, it follows that the center is round.
   2. It was said that the center is the place where lines reach their greatest perfection. Hence, it follows that the diametrical line is more perfect than any other line inside the circle.
   3. It was said that nowhere is the point as perfect as in the center. Hence, it follows that a round point is always more perfect than a long one.
   4. It was said that a figure cannot be without a center. Hence, it follows that every figure has an appetite for the center, and cannot exist without this appetite.
   5. It was said that not all centers are located here below. Hence, it follows that the sun, as well as the other planets, are the centers of their respective spheres.
   6. It was said that there is no locus as naturally desirable as the center. Hence, it follows that all points of angles have an appetite to move.
   7. It was said that the center is the place where the points of angles repose. Hence, it follows that all points of angles have an appetite to move.
   8. It was said that every center is common. Hence, it follows that some point is a genus that contains species.
   9. It was said that one who destroys the center also destroys motion. Hence, it follows that there is some center that cannot be moved.
  10. It was said that all secondary acts are likenesses of the center. Hence, it follows that every secondary center has moved.
