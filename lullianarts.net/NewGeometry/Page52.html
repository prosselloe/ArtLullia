2.2.10 Depth
Principles of Depth
   1. Depth is in the region of the center.
   2. The acute angle is the principle of depth.
   3. In every point in which there is an angle, there is depth.
   4. Without depth, no part can be in another part.
   5. Length and width are not as well disposed as depth to mixture and composition.
   6. Depth disposes parts to enter into each other, just as width disposes them to expand, and length disposes them in sequence.
   7. In the center of a square, where four diametrical lines meet, there must be 4 deep angles, and at the center of a pentagon where 5 diametrical lines meet, there must be 5 deep angles.
   8. Melancholy and depth are concordant.
   9. There is no element whose angle is as deep as earth's angle. 
  10. The sensitive powers all have more virtue for sensing depth than length or width.

Corollaries to the Principles of Depth
   1. It was said that depth is in the region of the center. Hence, it follows that appetite reposes in the center more in depth than in length or width.
   2. It was said that the acute angle is the principle of depth. Hence, it follows that depth is more consistent with the triangle than with the square.
   3. It was said that in every point in which there is an angle, there is depth. Hence, it follows that every point that has depth has discrete quantity.
   4. It was said that without depth, no part can be in another part. Hence, it follows that depth is the disposition, or gateway allowing parts to be within each other.
   5. It was said that length and width are not as well disposed to mixture and composition as depth. Hence, we conclude that depth is the primordial gate of generation.
   6. It was said that depth disposes parts to enter into each other, just as width disposes them to expand, and length disposes them in sequence. Hence, it follows that depth is an accident and not a substance.
   7. It was said that in the center of a square, where four diametrical lines meet, there must be 4 deep angles, and at the center of a pentagon where 5 diametrical lines meet, there must be 5 deep angles. Hence, it follows that every center has in itself one indivisible point which is the subject of many angles.
   8. It was said that melancholy and depth are concordant. Hence, we conclude that memory is the depth of science.
   9. It was said that no element has an angle as deep as earth's. Hence, it follows that in the earth's center, lines are closer to each other than in any other center.
  10. The sensitive powers all have more virtue for sensing depth than length or width. Hence, we conclude that the deepest sense impressions are the greatest.
We have dealt with the principles of Geometry and their corollaries, now we intend to deal with questions that can be made and solved with these principles and their corollaries. And as these are natural principles, we apply the questions to natural subjects.
