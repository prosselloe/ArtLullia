1.3.16 Fig 25 - the white circle
FIGURE OF THE WHITE CIRCLE
This figure is included in this science to provide a doctrine for considering how the white circle potentially holds points, straight and oblique lines, right, acute and obtuse angles, from which are derived the figures existing in the species of elemented corporeal substances, and you can consider these things in accordance with what we said about the figures of the degrees of elements, and other figures dealing with natural operations.
In the figure of the mixture of elements, we said that a. stands for fire that relates to air signified by b. and that their relation is in the concordance of heat and moisture, and this concordance causes a straight line and a right angle of quadrangular nature. And because fire instills its dryness into air, its influx causes an acute angle which is of a triangular nature, given that air and fire oppose each other through moisture and dryness.
And as air agrees with fire through heat, it declines from the right angle and makes an acute angle inasmuch as it restricts itself so that earth, the proper subject of dryness, cannot enter into it, and as air seeks help and sustenance, it inclines toward water which helps it against fire, and now it makes an obtuse angle which is confused because it is composed of a right angle and an acute one, and the remaining elements make angles in the same way as air, and generate circular, straight and oblique lines with the points that make them up, and bring them from potentiality into act.
This is how circular figures are made, when every element enters successively into every other element. With this figure, geometers can imagine the principles from which proceeds the oblique derivation of angles, of oblique, straight and circular lines with their constituent points, and the white circle potentially holds all these measurements through an artifice, and because it has these natures within its substance through natural agents, the artist draws to the surface of the circle the likenesses of lines, points and angles that are within.
At this point, there is much subject matter for philosophy, because here, many natural secrets are revealed to the human intellect that desires to consider subtly the likenesses which are brought from potentiality into act part by part so as to know the natural operations that elemented substances have in themselves within the course of nature.
