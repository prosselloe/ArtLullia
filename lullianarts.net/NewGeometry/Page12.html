1.3.1 Fig 7
THE THIRD PART
This part is about figures derived from the prime ones, here we show how some figures are derived from others, and how one can measure one figure with another; and we show how some parts are equivalent to others in one and the same figure.
Let us divide this figure, made of an equilateral triangle enclosed in a circle, into 5 equal parts which are equivalent to one another as the eye can see, and let us take a half of the triangle from this figure.
