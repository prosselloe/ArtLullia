1.3.10 Fig 19 - for measuring lunules
A FIGURE FOR MEASURING LUNULES


It is clear that the triangle in figure 16 potentially holds the division of the triangle shown in the present figure, divided into three equal parts signified by h.i.k., which are equivalent to houses e.f.g. as the eye can see.
And therefore, the triangle in the 16th figure with a. inscribed in it is worth 3 parts of the circle, and the 3 lunules b.c.d. of the same figure are worth more than a. inasmuch as the present figure has 6 lunules which the other potentially holds, and therefore, by this figure e.f.g.h.i.k. we can know the divisions that the other holds in potentiality, and by the divisions that the other figure potentially holds, the divisions of figure e.f.g.h.i.k can be brought from potentiality into act.
Through this figure, discovered by using the 16th figure, a doctrine can be given for knowing the extremities of circles, as in the 6 said lunules which make up the 7th part of the circle, and we will prove that this is so as follows: if house e. is one part of the heptagon, and house f. is another, and so on with g.h.i.k., and every part is equal to every other, while together they add up to the number 6, the number must necessarily increase to 7 on account of the lunules, so that the entire circle can be divided successively into equal parts; and we dealt with this in a similar way in the Major Geometry, in the 5th circle designated by the letter a. The six lunules therefore make up the seventh part of the whole circle, so that there be a succession of numbers from the 6th unit to the 7th, which could not be if the six lunules did not add up to the 7th part of the circle, because if they added up to the number 6, then house k. and the 6 lunules would both add up to one and the same number, which is impossible. And if they added up to an 8th part, the succession from 6 to 7 would be destroyed, but the natural numbering of equal parts cannot sustain any such destruction. And if the 6 lunules added up to one half-unit above k., the unity of numeric succession would be destroyed, inasmuch as there could be no integral increase of numbers by whole units like the one which occurs in the number made of e.f.g.h.i.k.
