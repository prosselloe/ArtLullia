1.3.6 Fig 13 - a circle
FIGURE OF A CIRCLE
This figure d.e.f. is included in this art to provide a doctrine whereby one can divide the circle into 3 essentially and substantially equal parts, and in this division we follow the method of the master figure and the method of the half triangle by drawing one diametrical line straight across the surface and dividing it into 8 double parts worth the 16 houses of the master figure of this art, and giving 3 of these 8 parts to d., 2 to e. and 3 to f., and this is because e. occupies a longer lengthwise space than d.f.
